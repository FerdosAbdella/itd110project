Global warming is the long term rise in the average temperature of the earth's climate system. This process raises levels of greenhouse gases, like carbon dioxide and methane, in the atmosphere. These gases create a layer that traps heat, leading to higher global temperatures. The effects of global warming are widespread, impacting the environment, ecosystems, and human life.

major impact is the change in weather patterns. As the climate warms, we experience more extreme weather events, such as stronger hurricanes, droughts, and heavy rains. These changes can harm natural ecosystems, threaten wildlife habitats, and lead to a loss of biodiversity, with many species struggling to adapt and facing extinction.

Global warming also poses serious risks to human health. Higher temperatures can worsen air pollution, causing respiratory issues, and heatwaves can lead to heat-related illnesses. Additionally, changes in climate can allow disease-carrying insects, like mosquitoes, to spread into new areas.

This website aims to educate the public about the causes and effects of global warming and to promote ways to reduce its dangers. We provide information on sustainable practices, renewable energy, and conservation efforts to empower individuals and communities to take action. Together, we can tackle the challenges of global warming and work towards a healthier, more sustainable future for ourselves and future generations.
